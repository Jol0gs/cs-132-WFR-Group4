Background

Poverty is one of the oldest and most pressing societal problems, and it is clearly evident in the Philippine context. Reducing poverty is a main goal of the United Nations' SDG 1 (No Poverty), which pushes for equal opportunities and a better quality of life for everyone. Despite years of development, poverty in the country is still closely tied to income inequality, inflation, regional differences, and employment challenges that affect many Filipino families.

To better understand and address these issues, national and international institutions like the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA), Asian Development Bank (ADB), and World Bank regularly track and publish data related to poverty. By combining and analyzing these datasets, we can gain clearer insights into the factors and trends behind poverty in the country and come up with more effective, data-driven solutions.

Research Questions

1. Trend Analysis: How does the poverty headcount ratio in the Philippines change over time?

2. Poverty Determinants: What demographic or economic factors (e.g., GDP per capita, employment rate) are correlated with changes in poverty levels?

3. Poverty Forecasting: How can recent trends of these factors be used to predict the poverty headcount for 2025?

Hypotheses

Null Hypothesis (H0): No significant correlation exists between the selected economic factors and poverty levels.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1): There is a significant correlation between the selected economic factors and poverty levels.

Data Collection & Repository

The team gathered data from both national and international sources that provide data on income, poverty incidence, country-level aggregates, and other economic indicators to ensure the reliability and comprehensiveness of our analysis. Primary sources included the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA), through its Poverty Statistics and Family Income and Expenditure Survey (FIES), complemented by datasets from the Asia Data Portal, Asian Development Bank (ADB), and Mendeley Data. These datasets were cleaned and combined to ensure consistency across indicators, helping us analyze poverty trends and the socioeconomic factors influencing them in the Philippines.
